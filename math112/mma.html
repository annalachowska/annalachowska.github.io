Mathematics 112

Mathematica Resources

To help build your problem-solving skills, and to introduce you to software important for many scientific applications, we shall make some use of Mathematica in this course.
With the aid of occasional in-class demonstrations, you will learn to use several simple commands with which you can explore an unlimited collection of examples.
In addition, you can use several more complicated pieces of Mathematica code, available on the course website, to build intuition about some of the more subtle issues of the course.
Mathematica is installed in all of the AM&T clusters. A list is available at http://www.yale.edu/acs/cluster/.
