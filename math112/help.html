Mathematics 112a

Where to go for help

The outside-of-class support for Math 112 has the following components:
    Coaching sessions (new this year),
    Instructor office hours,
    Quantitative reasoning tutors,
    Math and science tutors attached to the residential colleges,
    Peer tutors.
Coaching sessions Schedule TBA, Room TBA. Run by Wenyu Pan.
Coaching is a new support system for the students that will run this semester. It is designed especially for those in the class who need help the most. The participants of the coaching program will be assigned to a group of five or six, and each group will meet with a coach once a week for an hour. Sessions will be interactive, designed to help the students understand concepts currently taught in the course, and improve their problem solving skills. Coaching groups will be formed after the first midterm. If you are invited and accept the invitation, attendance becomes mandatory. If you miss more than one meeting without a very good excuse, your spot will be given to someone else. If you did not get invited, you can still apply - we will try to accommodate you depending on the availability of spots in the program. Schedules of group meetings will be coordinated by the coaches.
A limited number of individual tutoring slots by the coach will be available at the beginning of the semester before the regular coaching sessions begin.
Instructor office hours: Your instructor will hold weekly office hours. The information on the time and place should be available on your section webpage in classes*v2.
QR tutors and Math and Science tutors: The specialities and hours of availability for the math-science tutors and quantitative reasoning tutors can be checked at http://yalecollege.yale.edu/content/tutoring-and-academic-support.
Peer tutors:
Peer tutors are associated with Math112 only. They are very familiar with the course and the only students who come to their office hours are Math112 students, so the topics discussed are likely to be relevant to everyone. They can go over troublesome topics with you, and give homework hints. In addition, both peer tutors are available for individual appointments by request. You can email them for an appointment, just for yourself, or go in with a small group of friends.
