The Center of the Small Quantum Group

Ongoing research project



The small quantum group is a finite dimensional subquotient of the restricted quantized universal enveloping algebra, associated with a given root system and a primitive root of unity. It was defined by George Lusztig in the early 90s as an analog of the restricted enveloping algebra in prime characteristic (see here for the simply-laced case).

The first explicit computation of the center was done in 1995 in case of sl(2) by Thomas Kerler. In 1999, K.Brown and I.Gordon identified a natural subalgebra in the regular block of the center, isomorphic to the algebra of coinvariants of the given root system.

Here are some of my publications on the topic:

(1)On the Center of the Small Quantum Group - describes the largest known subalgebra in the center, the "twice-coinvariant algebra"
(2)The Small Quantum Group and the Verlinde Algebra
(3)The Small Quantum Group and the Springer Resolution (together with Roman Bezrukavnikov).- describes the center of the regular block of the small quantum group in terms of sheaf cohomologies over the Springer resolution. The "twice-coinvariant algebra" shows up nicely in this setting. However, the combinatorics of the whole center is not yet understood beyond the sl(2) case.

Currently we are working with Steve Jackson on a computation of the center of the small quantum sl(3). According to the preliminary results, the center of a small quantum group of rank higher than 1 is much bigger than the "twice-coinvariant" subalgebra found in (1). Still, we expect the answer to have a clear combinatorial description in terms of the given root system.

Hochschild cohomologies in the case sl(2)


Based on the geometric interpretation of the Hochshild cohomologies of the small quantum group, obtained in (3), together with Azat Gainutdinov we are studying the Gerstenhaber algebra structure on HH(u) in the case of the small quantum sl(2) . The result is likely to have implications for the W- algebra that is the symmetry of the (1,p) logarithmic conformal field theory (see paper)
